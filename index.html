Summary: Latitude vs.X

Background

The purpose of this project was to answer a question: "What's the weather like as we approach the equator?" Now, we know what you may be thinking:"Duh, It gets hotter...". But, if pressed, how would you prove it?

WeatherPy

At first, we created a Python script to visualize the weather of 500+ cities across the world of varying distance from the equator by utilizing OpenWeatherMap API. We created a series of scatter plots by using Matplotlib to showcase the following relationships:

  • Temperature(F) vs. Latitude
  • Humidity (%) vs. Latitude
  • Cloudiness(%) vs. Latitude
  • Wind Speed (mph> vs. Latitude

At last, we analyzed linear regression on each relationship in NortherHemisphere (greater than or equal to 0 degrees latitude) and Southern Heisphere (less than 0 degrees latitude).

In conclusion, we found statistically significant relation between maximum temperature and latitude, but we could not find any statistically significant relation between other parameter such as himidiy, cloudiness, and wind speed and latitude
